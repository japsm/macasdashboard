9. Special Go-Live Readiness & Hypercare Preparation

GS-side preparation sitting on top of the module-specific activities above — not configuration tasks, but the practices that make sure every deliverable actually gets produced and verified.

9.1 Go/No-Go Readiness Gate per Module — Owner: Japs, with the relevant GS module lead and MACAS counterpart. Timing: 2–3 business days before each go-live date. Output: a logged Go/No-Go decision, with conditional items carried into hypercare.
9.2 Production Dry Run (Not Just UAT Sandbox)
Re-run the matching UAT Guide checklist one more time in the actual production instance 1–2 days before each go-live — not the demo/training environment used during the June UAT.
9.3 Hypercare Staffing & Escalation Plan
Task 4.4 “Provide Go-Live Support” (Angel Abraham, Jul 7–28) exists but has no defined structure. Formalize who is on call each go-live week, response-time expectations, and an escalation path (module owner → Japs → BSB Board).
9.4 Rollback / Contingency Plan per Cutover
For Inventory and Payments especially — define the fallback in a one-page contingency note per module before go-live, not improvised after something breaks.
9.5 Quick-Reference Cards for Workaround Processes
Maintenance (hybrid Maintenance+Manufacturing, virtual BOMs per machine) and Production (by-products BOM, Route A/B, Rock Mass/BCM terminology) are workarounds and new terminology, not native Odoo flows. A one-page printable quick-reference for Moises' and Raymagean's teams would reduce user error in week one.
9.6 Daily War-Room / Stand-Up
15-minute daily check-in with the relevant module owner and MACAS counterpart during each go-live week, logged into the Activity & Decision Log.
9.7 Deliverables Checklist Tied to the Post-Implementation Report
Map each go-live task's stated deliverable (from the ProjectPlan) against actual production evidence — see Section 12 matrix.
9.8 Team Recognition / Momentum Markers
Publicly acknowledge in the GC the first live, end-to-end transaction per module. Maintain a simple visible go-live tracker per module that the team can watch fill in during the week.
✦ QA9.9 Go-Live Exception Log
A lightweight, module-tagged running list of issues hit during go-live week, kept separate from the formal Activity & Decision Log. Explicitly best-efforts and crowd-sourced: there is no single designated owner logging every exception. Any team member who spots an issue is responsible for clearly citing it as an exception so it can be reported and pattern-matched. The bar for inclusion is that the item is clearly flagged, not that it goes through formal sign-off.
✦ QA9.10 Manual Fallback / Continuity Check
Confirm and circulate a documented manual fallback process (e.g., paper DR, phone/text to dispatch or to Japs) for each module where a user being locked out of Odoo mid-transaction could otherwise stall real quarry operations.
✦ QA9.11 Note on Sign-Off (considered and dropped)
A formal per-report sign-off requirement was proposed and explicitly dropped. The team is not accustomed to formal sign-off procedures; ordinary collaboration and communication on an item is treated as sufficient indication of agreement.
